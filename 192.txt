North Dakota is a state located in the Midwest of the United States. It is the 19th largest state in terms of area and the third most backward in terms of population. Became part of the Union on November 2, 1889, becoming the 39th state.
The Missouri River flows through the state. The mountainous western region is rich in lignite coal and petroleum. The Red River, which flows through the eastern part, fertilizes the area. Agriculture has long been an important component of the state's economy.
Bismarck is the capital of North Dakota. Fargo is the largest city.
