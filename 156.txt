ECG Sudarshan or Ennakkal Chandy George Sudarshan was an Indian scientist who excelled in physics. His major contributions include the VAA theory of fatigue force, fundamental research in quantum optics, discoveries about open quantum systems, and theories about particles labeled 'tachyons' traveling faster than light. He settled in the United States and was fascinated by Indian philosophy. He has also lectured on Vedanta.
Sudarshan reveals that at a young age, he was fascinated by science when he saw the wheels inside his grandfather's clock at home, which his father had dropped down for oiling. 
He has been nominated for the Nobel Prize in Physics several times, but has not received it, citing the intervention of stakeholders in the scientific community. Sudarshan named Egon Krauss and Nobel laureate Murray Gellman among those who bought, copied and acquired prizes under his own name.
He was born on September 16, 1931, at Ennakkal House, Pallam, Pak, Kottayam District, Kerala. His father was EI Chandy Revenue Supervisor and his mother Achamma was a teacher. He passed away on May 14, 2018 at the age of 87 in the United States.
