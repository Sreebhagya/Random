Aung San Suu Kyi has been under house arrest for 15 years, from July 20, 1989, for leading the democratic uprising. Despite repeated calls from the international community and human rights groups for the release of Suu Kyi, the Myanmar military has agreed to release her on November 13, 2010.
Fighting for democracy in the Gandhian style, she is a Buddhist. Born in 1945, the daughter of Burmese freedom fighters General Aung San Suu Kyi and Mao Zedong, Suu Kyi was awarded the 1991 Nobel Peace Prize.
She was born on June 19, 1945, in Yangon, Burma, the daughter of General Aung San Suu Kyi and Mao Zedong, the Burmese freedom fighter and the father of modern Burma, Burma Gandhi. General Aung San was the man who founded the Burmese Independent Army and fought with the help of Japan to liberate it from British rule. On July 19, 1947, when Suu Kyi was two years old, General Aung San was shot dead along with four others. Burma became independent on January 4, 1948. Suu Kyi was educated at Methodist English Girls' High School. After her mother was appointed Ambassador to India in 1960, she moved to Delhi with her mother, Ma Kin Chi. Suu Kyi graduated from Lady Shri Ram College, Delhi in 1964. He later graduated from Oxford with a degree in economics, diplomacy and philosophy. In 1972, she married Michael Eris, a resident of Bhutan. She gave birth to her eldest son, Alexander, in 1973, and her youngest son, Kim, in 1977.
Myanmar gained full independence in 1948 and has been under military rule since 1962. She returned to Burma in 1988 to care for her ailing mother. Following the resignation of the then ruling Socialist Front leader, protests erupted across the country and the junta came to power. Inspired by Gandhi's principle of non-violence, he worked in the struggle against the junta. On September 27, 1988, the National League for Democracy was formed against the junta. On July 20, 1989, he was placed under house arrest by the military government on the condition that he "be released from the country". However, Suu Kyi refused to leave Burma.
The "National League for Democracy" won a landslide victory in the 1990 election, but the junta did not relinquish power. It received international attention and was awarded the 1991 Nobel Peace Prize by Suu Kyi. Alexander and Kim received the Nobel Prize for Suu Kyi. He was released from house arrest in 1995, but the junta's administration has stated that he will not be allowed to return to Burma to visit his family. 1997 - Michael Junta's government denies him a visa to return to London to care for his ailing husband. Suu Kyi never saw him again. Michael died in March 1999.
