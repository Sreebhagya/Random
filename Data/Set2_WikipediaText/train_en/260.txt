
Damoycelli beaks are three-and-a-half feet high beaks that fly as winter visitors to northern India. The body is pale white all over and the head and neck are black. They have red round eyes. There are feathers on the tail. Damoycelli beaks fly in groups with legs and neck extended. They have the characteristic of flying one after the other, like the V in English. They do not nest in India.
