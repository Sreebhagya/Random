Oberon is Uranus' outermost major satellite known as Uranus 4. It is the second largest satellite of Uranus and the ninth largest in the Solar System. Discovered in 1787 by William Herschel, the satellite is named after a character in Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night's Dream. Its orbit extends toward the outer surface of Uranus' magnetic field.
Oberon was discovered on January 11, 1787, by scientist William Herschelin. On the same day, he discovered Uranus' largest moon, Titania.
The moons of Uranus are named after characters created by William Shakespeare or Alexander Pope. Oberon derives its name from the character of Oberon, the king of fairies in William Shakespeare 's comedy Midsummer Nights Dream.
