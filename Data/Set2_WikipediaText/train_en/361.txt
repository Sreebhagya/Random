Axel Hyberg Island is an island in Nunavut, Canada, in the QuickTalk region. Situated in the Arctic Ocean, it is the 31st largest island in the world and the seventh largest in Canada. Statistics Canada estimates that the island covers an area of ​​43,178 square kilometers. The island is named in honor of Axel Hieberg.
One of the largest members of the Canadian Arctic archipelago, the island is a member of both the Swederp Islands and the Queen Elizabeth Islands. The island is known for its unusual fossil forests of the Eocene.
