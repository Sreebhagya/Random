The Punjab calendar is derived from the Bikrami calendar started by King Vikramaditya in 57 BC. This calendar is made by Surya Darshan and accordingly the first day of the calendar is the day of Vaishak, which is also the Punjabi New Year, which they celebrate as Vaishakhi.

The lunar calendar begins with the first day of the month, not the new year of the lunar year, but on the full moon day of the lunar month. The calendar begins and ends on the full moon day. The new year begins earlier. However, the Punjabi New Year is not the New Year in the lunar calendar, but the Lunar Calendar begins with the Punjabi Folk Poetry and Barah Maha.
