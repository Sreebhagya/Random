Tapo-Caparo National Park is a protected area in Venezuela. National Park is also known as Tapo Caparo.
The total area of ​​this national park is about 2,050 square kilometers. It is administratively located in the western part of Venezuela between the states of Barinas, Merida and Tachira. The national park was established on January 14, 1992 by order of the then President Carlos Andres Perez Government.
It is a diverse forest with many ridges and rivers, including junipers, mosses, mosses, fungi, and jaguars and taunts on wildlife. A dam called the Uribante-Caparo Hydroelectric Complex has been built near the park.
